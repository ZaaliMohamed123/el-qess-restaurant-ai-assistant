# El-Qess Restaurant - Frequently Asked Questions
## Reservations
### Do you take reservations?
Yes, we accept reservations and highly recommend them, especially for:
- Friday and Saturday evenings
- Groups of 6 or more
- Special occasions
You can make a reservation by calling us at (555) 789-2345, emailing reservations@elqess.com, or through our virtual assistant.
### How far in advance should I book?
For regular dining, 1-2 days in advance is usually sufficient. For weekends or groups of 8+, we recommend booking 3-5 days ahead. For private dining or special events, please book at least 1-2 weeks in advance.
### What is your cancellation policy?
Please cancel at least 4 hours before your reservation time. For groups of 10+, we require 24 hours notice. No-shows may be charged a fee for large party reservations.
### Do you have a grace period for reservations?
Yes, we hold reservations for 15 minutes past the booking time. Please call us if you're running late.
### Do you require a deposit for large groups?
Yes, groups of 10 or more require a deposit of $20 per person, which is applied to your final bill.
## Food & Menu
### Is your food halal?
Yes! El-Qess is 100% halal certified. All our meats are sourced from certified halal suppliers. This is very important to us.
### What is a tagine?
A tagine is both a traditional Moroccan clay cooking pot with a conical lid and the dish cooked inside it. The unique shape traps steam, creating incredibly tender and flavorful slow-cooked meals. Our tagines are cooked for hours to develop rich, complex flavors.
### What is couscous?
Couscous is tiny steamed balls of semolina wheat, a staple of Moroccan cuisine. At El-Qess, we hand-roll and steam our couscous fresh, serving it with aromatic broths, vegetables, and meats.

### Do you have vegan options?
Yes, many dishes can be prepared vegan. Our Zaalouk, Taktouka, and vegetable dishes are naturally vegan. Please inform your server of your dietary needs, and our kitchen will accommodate you.
### Do you have gluten-free options?
Yes! Several dishes are naturally gluten-free, including our tagines, grilled meats, and salads. Please let your server know about your gluten sensitivity, and they will guide you through safe options.
### Can you accommodate food allergies?
Yes, please inform your server of any allergies when ordering. Our kitchen uses nuts, dairy, gluten, and sesame. We take allergies seriously and will do our best to accommodate your needs.
### What is your most popular dish?
Our Couscous Royal ($28) is our signature dish and guest favorite. It features fluffy couscous with lamb, chicken, merguez sausage, and seven vegetables. The Tagine au Poulet ($22) with preserved lemons is also extremely popular.

## Moroccan Culture & Hospitality
### What does "El-Qess" mean?
"El-Qess" means "The Story" in Arabic. We believe every dish tells a story of tradition, family, and Moroccan heritage.
### What is Moroccan mint tea?
Moroccan mint tea, called "Atay," is a sweet green tea made with fresh mint leaves. It's a symbol of hospitality in Morocco and is traditionally poured from a height to create a frothy top. We serve it with every meal as a sign of welcome.
### What does "Marhaba" mean?
"Marhaba" means "Welcome" in Arabic. You'll hear our staff say it when you arrive!
### What does "Shukran" mean?
"Shukran" means "Thank you" in Arabic. We appreciate you dining with us!
